Food guru, TV star, writer and producer, author and chef Alton Brown and a big bushel of guests gather on the Cobb Energy Centre stage to celebrate the tenth anniversary of “Good Eats” on Food Network with a live variety show. During a fun-filled matinee and evening event, Brown sits down for a lively chat with fellow Food TV personality Ted Allen, who will riddle the GE host with questions posted by foodnetwork.com visitors. Standout scenes from the "Good Eats" 200 plus episodes will be screened, and various cast members will drop by.

Brown, who as a UGA grad and Marietta resident, has special status with Atlanta audiences, also faces a panel of "super fans" for a "Good Eats" trivia showdown.

There's more! Brown leads a live cooking demo (of a very peculiar nature) and a Q&A with the audience. "Good Eats" music director Patrick Belden performs live with his very small orchestra, and there is also a parade of props and puppets from the show as well as goofy costumes.

Brown is the author of several award-winning and New York Times Best Seller books including I’m Just Here for the Food and Feasting on Asphalt: The River Run. In addition to his own "Good Eats" show, he is involved in "Iron Chef America," "Search for the Next Iron Chef," "Next Food Network Star," "Feasting on Asphalt" and "Feasting on Waves."
